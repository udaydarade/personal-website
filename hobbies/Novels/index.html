"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee:Set in the racially charged atmosphere of the American South, this classic novel follows the moral awakening of young Scout Finch as her father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defends an innocent black man accused of rape.
"1984" by George Orwell:Orwell's dystopian masterpiece explores a totalitarian future where the government, led by Big Brother, controls every aspect of citizens' lives, presenting a chilling warning about the dangers of unchecked power.
"The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald: In the roaring twenties, Jay Gatsby's extravagant parties and his unrequited love for Daisy Buchanan are at the heart of this tale of wealth, obsession, and the American Dream, revealing the emptiness that can lie behind a facade of opulence.
